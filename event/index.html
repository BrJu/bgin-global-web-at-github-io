Blockchain Governance Initiative Network (BGIN)

A global network for blockchain stakeholders

Join our Online Meeting at June 18 Learn more about BGIN WG & SG Info

 

BGIN online meeting [13:00-15:00 UTC, June 18, 2020]

BGIN online meeting will be held on 13:00-15:00 UTC, June 18, 2020.

This is BGIN’s first public meeting where we will discuss key issues including the draft ToR of Governance Working Group and Identity & Privacy Enhancement Study Group, and strategies to develop the community base of BGIN. If you want to join the discussion, please fill in the registration form and submit by June 15. Discussion materials and Zoom link will be sent to the registered address. Contact us at bgin-contact@bgin.team if you have any questions.

And we make a call for agenda item and contributions. Please make a comment on the draft agenda of the meeting if you have any proposals for the items to be discussed at the meeting.

Date & Time:

13:00 - 15:00 UTC, June 18, 2020

Draft Annotated Agenda:

1. General logistical matters (Ryosuke Ushida) 5 min

Assistant to Acting Co-Chairs will introduce Agenda, Logistical Matters, Code of Conduct, and Note Well for the meeting. Members are expected to confirm the draft Code of Conduct and Note Well for the meeting.

(Documents)

  • Draft Code of Conduct
  • Draft Note Well

[Issues for Discussions]

  • Do you have any comments on the draft CoC and Note Well?

2. Welcome remarks (Mai Santamaria) 5 min.

3. Overview of BGIN and update from the March meeting (Shin’ichiro Matsuo) 20 min

Acting Co-Chairs will introduce Genesis of BGIN including purposes and core values as well as a current overview of the work program such as Working Group(WG) and Study Group(SG) They also will share feedback from the one-on-one meetings between them and initial contributors discussing potential areas for further considerations. The expected items covered in this session are listed below. Attendees are expected to share their views on the concept and current work program of BGIN as well as what they are expecting for BGIN to work on.

  • Genesis of BGIN
    • Background, Purpose and Core Values
    • Discussions at the inaugural meeting
    • About “governance” issue
    • Structure of the current BGIN: Working Group and Study Group
  • Communication Infrastructures
    • Mailing Lists
    • Web site
    • GitHub repository
  • Short-mid term roadmap
  • Feedback from the one-on-one meetings between Acting Co-Chair and Initial Contributors (*)

(Documents)

  • Draft Genesis of BGIN (pdf)
  • Draft TOR of BGIN (pdf)
  • Takeaways from the discussions among initial contributors so far

[Issues for Discussions]

  • What has brought you to BGIN? What do you expect from activities here?
  • Do attendees have any views on the core value and purposes of BGIN in the draft Genesis and TOR in light of your expectations?
  • Do you have any proposals on the process/modality of our meeting?
  • What are the roles of (acting) co-chairs, assistant and others? (This will be an input for Governance WG)

4. Governance WG (Aaron Wright and Shigeya Suzuki) 25 min

As agreed at the inaugural meeting in March, acting Co-Chairs of the Governance WG will introduce the draft TOR of the Governance WG including purposes and tentative work plan for the group. They also will suggest the way the group operates including communication tools (mailing list/Slack, etc.), editing mechanism (Github/Google docs, etc.), and potential additional roles (editors/secretariats, etc.) as well as a document format. Attendees are expected to share their views and ideas on the proposal and agree on the kick-off of the group.

(Documents)

  • Draft Charter/ToR [TBA]

[Issues for Discussions]

  • Do you agree on the purposes of the WG written in the draft? What should be the function of the WG, and the role of co-chairs and others(eg. editors)?
  • Do you have any preference for the communication tools, document format, and other logistical matters?
  • What are the potential work items of the WG? What are the essential items that should be in by-law of BGIN?
  • The WG will need more contributors. How should we enhance human resources?

5. ID/Privacy and Key Management SG (Nat Sakimura and Katharina Pistor) 25 min

Acting Co-Chairs of the ID/Privacy and Key Management SG will introduce the draft Charter/ToR of the SG including its purposes, scope and proposed deliverables. They also will suggest the way the group operates including communication tools (mailing list/Slack, etc.), editing mechanism (Github/Google docs, etc.), and potential additional roles (editors/secretariats, etc.) as well as a document format. Attendees are expected to express their thoughts on the proposal and to reach a consensus on the kick-off of the group. The draft is uploaded to the SG Github repository.

(Documents)

  • Draft Charter (pdf)

[Issues for Discussions]

  • Do you agree with the purposes and scopes of the SG?
  • Do you have any preference for the communication tools, document format, and other logistical matters?
  • What are the pain points of each stakeholder in identity and access management?
  • What are the potential work items and outputs at the SG?

6. Community Development (Shin’ichiro Matsuo) 20 min

Acting Co-Chairs will introduce the discussion note and summary of one-on-one meetings on community development strategy. Per the action points of the inaugural meeting in March, Robert Wardrop will share the updates on the reach out to public authorities in emerging markets and economies (EMEs). Attendees are expected to share their views and ideas on development strategies as well as proactively take roles to reach out to stakeholders.

[Issues for Discussions]

  • Collaboration with outside activities/organizations
  • How to increase the number of contributors from each stakeholder (engineers, businesses, regulators, etc.)
  • Public relations strategy

(Documents)

  • Discussion note on community development [TBA]
  • Takeaways from the discussions among initial contributors so far

7. Q&A on entire BGIN 5 min

Attendees may ask any questions on BGIN

8. Future Plan 5 min

Acting co-chairs will explain their tentative roadmap toward the 1st BGIN meeting (Block #1, this Fall). Attendees are expected to make comments on the proposal and agree on the short and mid-term plans.
In addition, acting co-chairs will call for contributors for BGIN’s administrative tasks (ex. logo, website sophistication)

[Issues for discussions]

  • Do members agree to hold Block #1 as an online meeting given the COVID-19 outbreak?
  • Should we seek for partnership/collocation with other events? Do members have suggestions on the specific events to reach out?
  • How many meetings are adequate? What about 3-4 times every year?
  • Is the frequency of WG meetings same as general meetings?

9. Closing remarks (Mai Santamaria) 5 min

Note: Time allocation may change due to additional agenda items. Each slot includes Q&A time.

(*) The summary document will be stored in the GitHub repository
